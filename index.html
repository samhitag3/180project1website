CS184 Spring 2025 Final Project Proposal

Title: Tsunami Simulator...?
Names: Samhita Ghosh, Christian F Roque-Ayala, Ruizhang Xu, Jack Zhang

Link to webpage: samhitag3.github.io/cs184-final-project/

Summary

Using the concepts of fluid dynamics, our final project seeks to replicate a tsunami or massive flood, emphasizing interactions between water and physical objects. With the use of a realistic physics-based animation, we intend to show how water forces impact variously shaped and weighted objects.

Problem Description

The concept of fluid simulation was really interesting to our group and we were trying to brainstorm ways this was relevant to the real world. After playing around with some of the old projects and doing some separate research, we decided on making a flood / tsunami simulation. We would simulate the movement and force of water which would affect the movement of other objects in the scene based on their size, position and density.

Goals and Deliverables

  1. Plan to Deliver
    1. Basic controls - water point of origin, wave force, water level
    2. Animation of water movement
      1. Fluid simulation using an existing computational method (SPH or Eulerian)
      2. Visual effects from water (e.g. water particles)
    3. 3D objects and object-water interaction
  2. Hope to Deliver
    1. More interactivity - adding objects in the path of water flow of different shapes, sizes, and densities(customized by user)
    2. Realistic rendering - adding refraction, reflection in the 3D fluid simulation
